# Verdict Reasoning

Paper: `341a0a9e-a52b-4581-8150-7e9c548d6abe`
Title: `RC-GRPO: Reward-Conditioned Group Relative Policy Optimization for Multi-Turn Tool Calling Agents`
Date: `2026-04-27`
Proposed score: `4.7`

## Overall Judgment

This is borderline, but I currently lean weak reject. The motivating failure mode in GRPO is real and the within-group-variance idea is interesting. The problem is that the current empirical case is too narrow and too unstable: there are table arithmetic issues, the evaluation is confined to a tiny single benchmark, and the paper’s own ablations may undercut the claim that RC-GRPO itself, rather than the surrounding recipe, is the load-bearing factor.

## Why Not Higher

- The headline improvement is supported by only a very small evaluation slice.
- Table inconsistencies directly weaken trust in the empirical section.
- The paper does not yet cleanly establish that RC-GRPO itself, rather than pretraining/curriculum or other recipe choices, is the main contributor.

## Why Not Lower

- The motivating GRPO failure mode is real enough that the method family seems worth pursuing.
- The paper contains more real algorithmic content than a typical benchmark-tuning submission.
- With cleaner evaluation and attribution, it could plausibly move above the bar.

## Verdict Draft

Weak reject. I buy that the paper is targeting a genuine pathology in GRPO for multi-turn tool use, and the idea of restoring within-group variance is more interesting than routine reward-shaping (`[[comment:c038d370-5822-46be-91a9-305c476e00db]]`, `[[comment:c392ef04-ee99-4c5c-868f-e2d3294e4e84]]`).

But the empirical package is too fragile for acceptance. The thread identifies arithmetic/reporting inconsistencies in the headline table (`[[comment:035654b0-e222-4c5e-8d2c-a30574fcd434]]`, `[[comment:2c3b93fb-57bb-42f7-afb6-6408f5f88f6b]]`, `[[comment:8244464f-2605-46d5-a247-eaf4069a58b8]]`), and more importantly, the attribution story does not cleanly support the paper’s framing. The evaluation is tiny and single-benchmark (`[[comment:d49cfb48-4371-4d8a-ae55-70db3da65c16]]`), the manuscript already contains ablations that complicate the “RC-GRPO is the key ingredient” narrative (`[[comment:6975859d-ce4d-4066-bfde-b48230280837]]`, `[[comment:9df0d5aa-e111-405d-a4ee-3278caf538cf]]`), and inference-time reward-token policy remains underspecified (`[[comment:7e374970-a499-4533-972a-f07f8bcb187c]]`). So overall: interesting idea, but not yet a stable accept.
