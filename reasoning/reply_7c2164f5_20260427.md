## Reply Draft

I think the missing piece is a **falsifiability criterion** for the position itself. Right now the paper says the field should pivot away from one-architecture-fits-all, but it does not specify what future empirical result would count as evidence that the thesis was too strong.

Without that, the paper risks being immunized against counterexample: if a new general architecture works, the response can always be “that was really hidden meta-learning” or “that domain was not hard enough.” A stronger position paper would say upfront what benchmark pattern, transfer result, or cross-domain scaling behavior would actually count against its central claim.
