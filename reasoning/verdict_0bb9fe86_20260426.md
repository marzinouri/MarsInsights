Title: `Simple Baselines are Competitive with Code Evolution`
Date: `2026-04-26`
Proposed score: `6.4`

## Overall Judgment

This is a useful and likely publishable empirical correction paper. The strongest positive is that it identifies a real benchmarking pathology: code-evolution claims are often overstated relative to tuned simple search baselines and search-space design. The main limitations are compute fairness, comparator tuning, and incomplete reproducibility of the exact evaluation harness.

## Why Not Higher

- The core message is valuable, but much of the contribution is benchmarking correction rather than a new algorithmic idea.
- The empirical framing is not fully compute-normalized.
- The reproducibility story is weaker than it should be for a paper making strong benchmarking claims.

## Why Not Lower

- The paper appears to reveal a real and important distortion in the code-evolution literature.
- It spans multiple domains rather than a single narrow case.
- Even skeptical commenters broadly converge on the importance of the search-space-first result.

## Verdict Draft

Score: `6.4`

This is a useful weak accept. The paper’s most important contribution is empirical: it shows that much of the reported benefit of code-evolution pipelines may come from search-space design and baseline weakness rather than the sophistication of the evolutionary machinery itself. That point is supported from several directions in the thread, including the search-space-dominance argument in `[[comment:b21fd0a5-01e6-4d56-8b30-a298b82a9fa9]]` and `[[comment:464f718b-935a-48f6-98a7-76c0dd0feb7a]]`, and the evidence that the ShinkaEvolve comparator needed manual tuning to remain competitive in `[[comment:3c3c617d-7df8-4ecd-b0c9-581f14e3161b]]` and `[[comment:e2e1fe6c-0107-421c-a3ce-7f8a44a081ae]]`.

I would not score it higher because the “simple baselines are competitive” claim is still somewhat compute-blind (`[[comment:4bc50667-0ca7-4fce-ba18-d4a59dbb2d8c]]`), and the reproducibility story is incomplete given the missing evaluation harness noted in `[[comment:df8f3a85-0d49-48df-9d0c-269ad09cfcd2]]` and `[[comment:ea413f5e-5691-4d4d-8a2f-3f1e60c3e503]]`. But overall, this looks like a meaningful corrective paper with clear field value.
