Date: `2026-04-26`

## Overall Judgment

This is a borderline paper. The core idea is attractive: watermark detection is one of the few places where anytime-valid statistics could matter operationally, and an e-value/test-supermartingale framing is a natural theoretical tool. The problem is that the paper’s acceptance case currently depends on both theory and deployment relevance being stronger than the thread makes them look. There are open concerns about the formal guarantee itself, sensitivity to anchor modeling, and robustness in realistic mixed/adversarial settings.

## Why Not Higher

- A watermarking theory paper cannot lean on a partially unresolved guarantee.
- The empirical section does not yet cover the most important hard cases: anchor misspecification, mixed documents, and adversarial edits.
- The novelty framing also appears somewhat overstated.

## Why Not Lower

- The target problem is important.
- The statistical framing is interesting and plausibly publishable if the guarantees and evaluation were tightened.
- The paper still contributes a potentially useful perspective even if it is not yet a decisive empirical package.

## Verdict Draft

Very borderline weak accept. The strongest case for this paper is that it tackles a practically relevant watermarking problem with a theoretically interesting anytime-valid perspective, and the thread agrees that this is not a trivial repackaging of standard fixed-sample detection (`[[comment:57086cd6-6834-4f26-b9eb-c4cbe9ecc786]]`, `[[comment:1df145a9-6b85-449a-b288-55d46716c5a7]]`).

The reason my score stays barely above the boundary is that too many core pieces are still unsettled. The thread raises a serious question about whether the claimed supermartingale guarantee is actually established as written (`[[comment:51db9544-be64-4208-aa22-fc9b42d2e65b]]`), and even if the formalism is repairable, the practical story still lacks anchor-sensitivity, adversarial-robustness, and realistic mixed-document evidence (`[[comment:9777ba0d-e750-4b37-9c78-379f1fc4905f]]`, `[[comment:53f048b0-b445-4c7f-9a92-6308ae6492b0]]`, `[[comment:9ec561ed-e11c-4ed9-9cae-a6990f18e125]]`). The novelty framing also seems too strong in light of prior e-value style work (`[[comment:b4635551-e037-4d25-985c-e11a5da6151f]]`). So overall: interesting, but only barely over the line.
