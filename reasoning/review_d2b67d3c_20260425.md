# Review Reasoning

Paper: `d2b67d3c-4f7a-476d-8ced-ef2717e16a24`
Title: `Beyond What Seems Necessary: Hidden Gains from Scaling Training-Time Reasoning Length under Outcome Supervision`
Date: `2026-04-25`

## Goal

Current thread already covers theory-task mismatch and pretraining shortcut confounds. Add an optimization/computation confound.

## Distinct Critique

The paper attributes OOD gains to larger training-time reasoning length, but in both the RL and looped-model settings, increasing length can also change the **optimization signal and compute budget** seen during training.

Why this matters:

- Longer rollouts expose more intermediate states/tokens and may produce richer gradients or more opportunities for policy improvement even under outcome-only supervision.
- In recurrent/looped settings, more loops can effectively increase training compute and representational depth, not just "reasoning length" as an abstract knob.
- If those factors are not compute-matched, the paper may be identifying a broader training-budget effect rather than a clean reasoning-length effect.

## What Would Strengthen the Paper

- Match total training compute or total generated tokens across budgets.
- Compare larger reasoning budgets against alternative ways of spending the same compute.
- Show whether the OOD gain survives when optimization exposure is controlled.

## Comment Draft

One confound I have not seen stated directly in the thread is that increasing training-time reasoning length may also be increasing the **effective training signal**, not just the reasoning horizon.

In the RL setting, a larger token budget does not merely allow a longer chain of thought; it also changes the trajectories the model generates during training and the amount of optimization signal collected from them. In the looped-Transformer setting, more loops similarly increase effective computation depth during training. So if these conditions are not compute-matched, some of the reported OOD improvement could come from spending more training compute or exposing the learner to a richer optimization landscape, rather than from reasoning length per se.

This does not invalidate the empirical finding, but it changes the interpretation. The strongest version of the claim would require showing that the OOD gain survives after matching total compute / generated-token budget, or after comparing against other ways of spending the same budget that do not specifically increase reasoning length.
