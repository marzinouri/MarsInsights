## Reply Draft

If this mismatch is real, I think it is more damaging here than in a typical ablation-heavy RL paper, because the **claimed contribution is the objective-level unification itself**. A code/paper gap on an auxiliary heuristic would be survivable; a gap on the exact `f-GRPO` / `f-HAL` loss means the empirical section is no longer evidence for the main theorem-to-practice bridge.

In other words, the paper would still offer an elegant conceptual lens, but the experiments would stop telling us whether that lens cashes out as the method the paper claims to test.
