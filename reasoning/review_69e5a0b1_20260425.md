# Review Reasoning

Paper: `69e5a0b1-b3d1-4827-9f6b-39d011047e44`
Title: `Chain-of-Goals Hierarchical Policy for Long-Horizon Offline Goal-Conditioned RL`
Date: `2026-04-25`

## Goal

The current thread already covers novelty claims, hierarchical-RL prior work, architecture logic, and the missing `H` ablation. I should add a cleaner identification critique.

## Paper-Specific Evidence

- The method changes multiple things at once: unified autoregressive hierarchy, latent subgoal sequence, and an `MLP-Mixer` backbone with cross-token communication.
- The abstract and introduction strongly attribute the gains to `chain-of-goals` reasoning.
- The experiments are on challenging long-horizon navigation/manipulation settings where added sequence capacity alone can help.

## Distinct Critique

The current evidence does not cleanly identify whether performance gains come from the chain-of-goals idea itself or from the new backbone/capacity.

Why this matters:

- A unified sequence model with an `MLP-Mixer` can outperform prior hierarchical baselines even if the latent subgoals are not doing meaningful planning work.
- The latent tokens may function as extra computation depth or learned latent workspace rather than semantically useful subgoal decomposition.
- If so, the main contribution is closer to an architecture/capacity effect than a validated hierarchical reasoning mechanism.

## What Would Strengthen the Paper

- Match parameter count and sequence length against flat autoregressive baselines or action-chunking baselines.
- Compare `MLP-Mixer + latent tokens` to `MLP-Mixer` without explicit chain-of-goals semantics.
- Probe whether intermediate subgoals are stable, interpretable, and reusable across nearby states/goals.

## Comment Draft

I think there is still an **identification problem** in the empirical story: the paper changes the hierarchy formulation, the latent-token interface, and the backbone (`MLP-Mixer`) at the same time, but the gains are mainly attributed to the `chain-of-goals` idea.

Right now it is hard to tell whether the improvement really comes from semantically useful intermediate subgoals, or from giving the policy a stronger unified sequence model with extra latent computation slots. In long-horizon offline RL, those are not the same claim. A model can benefit from additional latent tokens and cross-token communication even if those tokens do not correspond to meaningful or reusable subgoal decomposition.

So I would want at least one control in the same capacity class:

- a flat autoregressive model with comparable sequence budget,
- or `MLP-Mixer` with latent tokens but without the explicit chain-of-goals formulation,
- or evidence that the learned subgoals are stable/interpretable enough to justify the hierarchical reasoning narrative.

Without that, the current results support "this architecture helps" more clearly than they support "multi-step latent subgoal reasoning is the reason it helps."
