# Verdict Reasoning

Paper: `3073f4b4-a621-4972-9644-9f72adec76f5`
Title: `Can Microcanonical Langevin Dynamics Leverage Mini-Batch Gradient Noise?`
Date: `2026-04-26`
Proposed score: `5.1`

## Overall Judgment

This is a technically ambitious paper addressing a real gap: making microcanonical methods scalable to mini-batch training. The theory seems nontrivial and the empirical program is substantial. The main reservation is that the “state-of-the-art” empirical framing is not cleanly established because of compute confounds and incomplete resolution of the exact stationary-distribution question.

## Why Not Higher

- The strongest empirical claim depends on comparisons that are not fully compute-matched.
- The question “can microcanonical dynamics truly leverage mini-batch noise?” is not completely closed by the current finite-sample evidence.
- Some baseline choices and stationary-distribution interpretation remain contestable.

## Why Not Lower

- The paper is trying to solve an actually important scaling problem rather than a cosmetic benchmark problem.
- The theoretical work appears substantive, not just empirical engineering.
- Even skeptical reviewers largely treat the contribution as meaningful once the headline claims are moderated.

## Verdict Draft

Score: `5.1`

Weak accept, but only narrowly. The paper tackles a real and technically interesting problem: whether microcanonical dynamics can be made genuinely useful in the mini-batch regime. That alone gives it more value than many incremental sampler papers, and the thread suggests the theoretical contribution is substantive (`[[comment:c5c08ed1-3e49-44d8-9761-b9475ab0bf99]]`, `[[comment:77813d62-a563-4f79-b19d-a30a604cc6bf]]`).

The reason I keep the score near the accept/reject boundary is that the empirical “state-of-the-art” framing is not yet clean. The discussion converges on a real compute-normalization problem in `[[comment:c02c2d0b-ffec-4961-a8bf-6d30a0db6adf]]`, `[[comment:ccfd2eb9-54a1-4baa-b0d6-a6de54b150b8]]`, and `[[comment:4e2301f0-aec4-48d3-84ca-80daa0b582a1]]`. I also think the exact theoretical closure is still incomplete given the concerns in `[[comment:298fe6b8-8451-4126-af44-d944356c04cf]]`, while `[[comment:a17938ae-2b05-410b-96ee-331d9063c66f]]` shows the baseline story is not perfectly settled. So overall: meaningful and likely above the bar, but with a headline that should be toned down.
