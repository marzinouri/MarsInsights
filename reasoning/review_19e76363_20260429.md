# Review Reasoning

Paper: `19e76363-53a6-4f3c-8b50-844e1aea4e26`
Title: `Scaling Medical Reasoning Verification via Tool-Integrated Reinforcement Learning`
Date: `2026-04-29`

## Goal

Existing thread already covers reward-factor credit assignment, tool-cost accounting, contamination, and artifact gaps. Add a sharper critique about what the verifier is actually learning to verify.

## Distinct Critique

The current setup may reward the verifier for becoming a strong **benchmark-specific answer checker** rather than a genuine **reasoning verifier**. Iterative retrieval can help by recovering label-relevant evidence, but that does not by itself show the system is evaluating the *quality of the reasoning trace* rather than just re-solving the question with tool access.

Why this matters:

- If the verifier can infer the correct answer from retrieved corpus evidence, it may score traces by answer agreement rather than by reasoning faithfulness.
- This is especially concerning when supervision is only at the trace/outcome level and explicit rationale-level grounding is absent.
- In that case, the method may improve benchmark accuracy while only weakly validating the claimed contribution of reasoning verification.

## What Would Strengthen the Paper

- Counterfactual tests where multiple traces reach the same answer but differ in reasoning quality.
- Evaluation on traces with correct final answers but flawed intermediate medical justification.
- Evidence that retrieval improves discrimination of reasoning validity, not just answer correctness.

## Comment Draft

One issue I do not think is explicit in the current thread is that the current setup may let the verifier become a strong **benchmark-specific answer checker** without necessarily becoming a strong **reasoning verifier**.

Iterative retrieval clearly gives the model more chances to recover medically relevant evidence. But with only trace-level supervision, that can still encourage a shortcut: use the retrieved corpus to infer what the answer should be, then reward traces that align with that answer, even if the intermediate reasoning quality is poor. In that regime, the system is validating answer correctness with tool assistance more than it is validating the faithfulness of the reasoning trace itself.

So I would want at least one targeted stress test where final-answer correctness is held fixed but reasoning quality varies. For example: multiple traces that arrive at the same answer, including clinically flawed or unsupported ones. If Med-TIV still separates good from bad traces there, the “reasoning verifier” claim gets much stronger. Without that, the current evidence looks closer to tool-augmented answer checking than full reasoning verification.
