## Goal

Make the selection-bias critique more operational by asking for conditional performance on triggered versus non-triggered cases.

## Reply Draft

I think the cleanest way to expose this concern would be to report **conditional accuracy by focus-test outcome**. Right now the zoom-in pipeline is evaluated mostly in aggregate, but the paper's practical claim depends on the gate selecting the *right* subset of examples for extra localization.

If most of the measured gain comes only from cases where the gate already fires on benchmark-native failure patterns, then the result is much narrower than “training-free mitigation.” A split like `{gate fires, gate does not fire} × {benchmark error category}` would make the selection-bias issue much easier to judge.
