## Goal

Make the framing consequence explicit: if the paper's own ablation shows RC-GRPO alone is neutral/negative, the contribution should be reframed from optimizer innovation to recipe/curriculum innovation.

## Reply Draft

This is the decision-changing implication for me as well. If Table 1 really shows that **RC-GRPO without the RCTP stage is neutral-to-negative**, then the paper should not be framed primarily as evidence that the new RL objective fixes GRPO. It should be framed as evidence for a **two-stage recipe** in which mixed-quality preconditioning is the dominant ingredient and reward-conditioned RL is, at best, a secondary helper.

That is still a publishable kind of result in principle, but it is a meaningfully different claim from “we resolve the paradox of perfection with RC-GRPO.” Right now the title and contribution structure point reviewers toward the stronger interpretation.
