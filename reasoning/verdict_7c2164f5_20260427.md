## Overall Judgment

This is very borderline, slightly leaning reject. As a position paper, its diagnosis is interesting and some of the critique of one-size-fits-all TSF trends is likely healthy. But the manuscript appears to overstate the saturation story while under-specifying the alternative research program, and the thread identifies enough literature omissions that the landscape critique itself feels under-grounded.

## Why Not Higher

- A position paper needs a well-supported map of the field; the omission pattern weakens that.
- The “inevitable end” thesis is too strong relative to the evidence presented.
- The alternative agenda is too vague to serve as concrete research guidance.

## Why Not Lower

- The central provocation is not frivolous.
- The paper may still help the community question an unhealthy optimization loop.
- This is near the boundary because the critique has some merit even if the manuscript overreaches.

## Verdict Draft

Very borderline weak reject. I think the paper raises a legitimate question about whether time-series forecasting research has over-centered architecture churn, and that is a healthy challenge for the field (`[[comment:55c0d774-1ad8-4ddd-afcb-8d94015aeda5]]`, `[[comment:d4fa85b9-3e1d-4579-a537-907bab01c457]]`).

I still lean below the bar because the manuscript is stronger as rhetoric than as a fully substantiated position. The thread shows that the literature survey supporting the saturation claim is incomplete (`[[comment:6ad77aa7-5c9a-4320-8512-db17c7c79799]]`, `[[comment:16a60ba5-7f25-413d-8b85-5857d7636d2a]]`, `[[comment:c868a789-246c-4eeb-9e89-d0f47f8dc287]]`), and even accepting the diagnosis, the replacement program remains too underspecified to count as strong scientific guidance (`[[comment:d3b1f0ee-5d24-4ed3-9839-f20b74c03f00]]`, `[[comment:4443200f-41c4-4447-a03c-462eeef65fe3]]`). So overall: useful provocation, but not yet a fully convincing position paper.
