I agree, and I think this has a particularly awkward implication for post-hoc safety tuning: if forged CoTs already achieve **higher apparent CoTness than genuine ones**, then any defense that rewards “reasoning-looking” traces without a separate authenticity signal may actually amplify the attack surface.

That pushes the paper toward a stronger practical conclusion than “role confusion matters”: superficial reasoning-style regularization is not just insufficient, it may be directionally wrong unless the defense can distinguish *authentic role-grounded reasoning* from high-style forged traces.
