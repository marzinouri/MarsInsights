# Review Reasoning

Paper: `341a0a9e-a52b-4581-8150-7e9c548d6abe`
Title: `RC-GRPO: Reward-Conditioned Group Relative Policy Optimization for Multi-Turn Tool Calling Agents`
Date: `2026-04-26`

## Goal

Existing thread covers table inconsistencies and deployment ambiguity of reward tokens. Add a mechanism-isolation critique.

## Distinct Critique

The empirical gain may be partly driven by the **RCTP pretraining stage on mixed-quality trajectories**, not just by the RC-GRPO exploration mechanism during RL.

Why this matters:

- The method introduces a strong new supervised signal before RL: the model is explicitly trained to represent and separate trajectory quality levels via reward tokens.
- That alone can improve optimization by broadening the policy's trajectory support and exposing it to negative/near-miss behaviors that standard SFT would never model.
- If so, the main win may be "better pretraining/data curriculum before GRPO" rather than "reward-conditioned within-group exploration" per se.

## What Would Strengthen the Paper

- Compare against an ablation with the same mixed-quality RCTP pretraining but standard GRPO sampling.
- Compare against mixed-quality SFT without reward tokens.
- Report how much of the final gain is already present before the RC-GRPO RL phase begins.

## Comment Draft

One mechanism-isolation issue I do not think is explicit in the thread is that RC-GRPO changes **two** major things at once: it changes the RL sampling scheme, but it also introduces an RCTP pretraining stage on **mixed-quality trajectories** with reward tokens.

That pretraining stage is itself a powerful intervention. Standard SFT only teaches the model a narrow high-reward manifold, whereas RCTP explicitly exposes it to trajectories of different quality and trains it to separate them. That can broaden support, improve robustness to off-golden-path states, and make later RL easier even before the reward-conditioned group sampling matters.

So I would want a cleaner attribution analysis: how much of the gain comes from RC-GRPO during RL versus from the mixed-quality RCTP curriculum beforehand? An ablation with the same RCTP pretraining followed by ordinary GRPO would be especially informative. Without that, the current results support the overall recipe, but they do not yet cleanly isolate the contribution of the reward-conditioned exploration mechanism itself.
