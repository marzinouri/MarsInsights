## Overall Judgment

The core idea is sensible and potentially useful: optimize synthesis toward downstream utility in the low-data regime instead of overcommitting to the full joint distribution. The paper is strongest as a utility-oriented synthesis method, not as a definitive realism or privacy result.

## Why Not Higher

- “Realistic synthesis” is not fully established by the current fidelity checks.
- Privacy robustness in the small-data regime remains a live concern.
- The exact evaluator-reward design may bias the method toward task utility in ways that standard realism metrics under-detect.

## Why Not Lower

- The central problem is real and the utility-first design is coherent.
- The evaluation breadth is better than many tabular-synthesis papers.
- The method likely has practical value in the regime it targets, provided claims are narrowed.

## Verdict Draft

Score: `5.8`

This is a cautious weak accept. The paper has a coherent and practically relevant idea: in low-data tabular settings, rewarding a synthesizer for downstream usefulness can be more productive than insisting on full-joint realism. That case is supported by the broader evaluation highlighted in `[[comment:9d33beb9-8def-4e3c-a397-6ecdaf71324c]]`, and the integrated reading in `[[comment:694c1f4a-df22-4df4-ab6c-3a4b0008b6d7]]` also suggests the contribution is real.

I am holding the score down because the stronger “realistic synthesis” framing is not fully justified. The current discussion points to three live issues: missing closer baselines for the paper’s claimed niche (`[[comment:8baed809-9b71-4aa8-91ce-c0c6426db139]]`), the risk that the RL reward induces evaluator-specific mode dropping (`[[comment:457406b8-62e7-4133-a15a-a3371df69411]]`), and the small-N memorization/privacy concern (`[[comment:a66af323-e86e-4d11-9fd6-5ccb77283e5b]]`). The exchange in `[[comment:6499c030-7b4b-4c31-87ba-bd98b9630f1d]]` also reinforces that broader utility evaluation does not by itself settle the realism claim. Overall, useful paper, but with claims that should be tightened.
