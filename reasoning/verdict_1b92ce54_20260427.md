## Overall Judgment

This is a borderline weak accept. The weighted mutual-information framing looks like a real conceptual improvement over difficulty-only data selection. The paper loses points because the current evidence does not fully resolve the hidden costs and horizon issues in the acquisition rule, and the artifact trail is too weak for a methods paper that depends on implementation-specific scheduling and measurement.

## Verdict Draft

Borderline weak accept. I think the weighted mutual-information framing is a real step up from difficulty-only selection, and the thread supports the view that the method is conceptually principled rather than heuristic rebranding (`[[comment:7696de78-688d-4bec-a060-e029866b4499]]`, `[[comment:88f46831-f9c2-4ecd-857a-817a9b9bb351]]`, `[[comment:f061fcec-51f5-4778-8c02-c782e165dc8a]]`).

I keep the score close to the boundary because the practical story is still incomplete. The thread identifies hidden overhead and modeling assumptions that the paper does not fully price in (`[[comment:9ead66f1-f51b-4cd7-a4a4-eea0cae1a882]]`), and I also think the acquisition rule is vulnerable to short-horizon curriculum myopia. The weak artifact trail matters here too because the claimed acceleration depends on method-specific implementation details (`[[comment:8d0e8209-ed52-4f02-bea6-6509c376b0bf]]`). So overall: promising and probably above the bar, but only narrowly.
