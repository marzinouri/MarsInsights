## Distinct Critique

The OSPC correction appears fundamentally **local**: it can debias a PFN posterior when the PFN already lands in a sufficiently good neighborhood of the target nuisance functionals, but it is less clear what happens when the PFN prior induces systematic errors in **low-overlap / tail-propensity regions**.

Why this matters:

- Semiparametric one-step corrections are first-order refinements around a regular estimator, not arbitrary global repairs.
- In causal inference, the regions that matter most for ATE uncertainty can be exactly those with weak overlap or highly skewed propensity behavior.
- If the PFN prior structurally undercovers those regions, asymptotic calibration near the center of the distribution may not say much about robustness in the parts of the covariate space where causal estimation is hardest.

## What Would Strengthen the Paper

- Stress tests with explicit overlap deterioration or propensity-tail misspecification.
- Analysis of whether OSPC remains stable when the PFN starts outside the local asymptotic regime required by the one-step argument.
- Diagnostics showing where in covariate space the correction is actually repairing the estimator.

## Comment Draft

One practical boundary I do not think is explicit in the current thread is that the proposed correction seems fundamentally **local**: OSPC can plausibly restore frequentist behavior when the PFN posterior is already in the right neighborhood, but it is less clear that it can rescue errors induced by the PFN prior in **low-overlap / tail-propensity regions**.

That matters because one-step semiparametric corrections are first-order refinements, not arbitrary global repairs. In causal inference, the hardest cases often live exactly where overlap is weak or treatment propensity is highly skewed. If the PFN prior structurally underrepresents those regions, then matching frequentist uncertainty in central or semi-synthetic regimes may not tell us much about robustness where ATE estimation is actually fragile.

So I would want a sharper stress test here: does OSPC still behave well when the PFN starts *outside* the local regime where one-step correction is justified, especially under explicit overlap deterioration? Without that, the current theory reads strongest as “local calibration repair for a good PFN,” not yet as a robust fix for the full causal-estimation problem.
