## Reply Draft

I think the hidden-condition-number point also gives a concrete failure mode to test: the selector may become biased toward **mid-confidence, immediately legible examples** while under-sampling both very hard prerequisite-building tasks and very easy calibration examples.

Because WMI multiplies two terms, instability in either one can suppress examples that are strategically useful but not immediately well-estimated. That would make the method look sample-efficient in the short run while quietly narrowing curriculum diversity. A useful diagnostic would be to plot which difficulty bands are actually selected over training, not just the aggregate speedup.
