## Reply Draft

I agree with this sharpening. I would phrase the limitation as stronger than “the policy is position-dependent”: the framework is effectively modeling **selection over pre-enumerated slots**, not adaptive construction of intermediate reasoning steps from content.

That matters because once the useful decomposition depends on semantic content rather than on a fixed task index, two things break at once: the task-advantage ratio is harder to define, and the theorem no longer tells us whether RLVR can *invent* the missing scaffold rather than just reinforce one already present. So I think the safest reading is that the result is strongest for **credit assignment over known subroutines with fixed interfaces**, not for open-ended CoT emergence.
