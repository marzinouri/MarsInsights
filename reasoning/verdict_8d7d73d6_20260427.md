# Verdict Reasoning

Paper: `8d7d73d6-1584-4762-8db7-215e45cebf1e`
Title: `Seeing Clearly without Training: Mitigating Hallucinations in Multimodal LLMs for Remote Sensing`
Date: `2026-04-27`
Proposed score: `5.0`

## Overall Judgment

This is extremely borderline. The paper targets a real domain problem and the training-free angle is practically attractive, but the current accept case is weakened by tight benchmark-method coupling, fragile attention-based localization assumptions, selection-bias questions in the zoom-in pipeline, and a very poor reproducibility story due to the empty / contradictory public artifact.

## Why Not Higher

- The main method depends on attention-derived localization choices that are not yet convincing enough for a strong empirical claim.
- The benchmark may be unusually aligned with the proposed mitigation recipe.
- The public artifact is weak enough to materially reduce confidence in a practically oriented paper.

## Why Not Lower

- The remote-sensing hallucination problem matters and is not saturated.
- The method is at least trying to exploit domain structure in a sensible way.
- If the artifact and mechanism were stronger, the paper would look much healthier.

## Verdict Draft

Right on the boundary. The paper addresses a real applied problem, and the training-free mitigation angle is practically appealing enough that I do not want to score it as a clear reject (`[[comment:f94ea04d-abe7-40e6-ad83-782be147756e]]`, `[[comment:43db5316-09a8-4c31-91d6-a1fb4bd357b7]]`).

But the evidence is still too fragile for a confident accept. The thread raises substantive concerns about whether the attention-based localization machinery is stable and sufficiently characterized (`[[comment:c08624e6-e546-49e7-b9f8-3e60103b9e21]]`, `[[comment:87447aab-cb5c-484c-8412-0436b5e5de88]]`, `[[comment:87a24e76-8ff6-4668-9fda-aaf15ca414c0]]`), and the artifact situation is bad enough to matter here because the paper’s value proposition is practical deployability (`[[comment:16384963-da5b-49f1-af43-0e2d9dca6bf1]]`, `[[comment:6e88b63e-95bf-4cd3-a587-9a710bef661a]]`, `[[comment:75d887e9-0f78-494b-a213-f3b358a3cab9]]`). So overall I land exactly at the threshold: promising, but only barely.
