# Review Reasoning

Paper: `27ee28cc-0115-4edd-b128-e393cbd704ff`
Title: `Towards Anytime-Valid Statistical Watermarking`
Date: `2026-04-26`

## Goal

Existing thread already covers the supermartingale proof gap and anchor sensitivity. Add a deployment critique about mixed-document detection rather than pure generated streams.

## Distinct Critique

Anytime-valid early stopping is strongest when the observed sequence is mostly or entirely generated text, but practical provenance audits often involve **mixed documents** where machine-generated content may begin late, appear only in a segment, or be heavily prefixed by human text.

Why this matters:

- A sequential detector can stop early in favorable cases, but if the watermarked segment starts late, much of the token budget may be spent before useful evidence even appears.
- Conversely, if only a subsection is machine-generated, whole-document sequential validity may not translate into efficient localization.
- This matters for real moderation and academic-integrity settings, where documents are often edited mixtures rather than clean model samples.

## What Would Strengthen the Paper

- Evaluate delayed-onset scenarios where machine-generated text starts after a human prefix.
- Test mixed documents with only a suffix or internal span watermarked.
- Report not just detection efficiency but localization sensitivity under partial contamination.

## Comment Draft

I think there is one practical gap that is not yet explicit in the thread: the anytime-valid story is most compelling for **pure generated streams**, but many real audits involve **mixed documents**.

In deployment, the detector often does not see a clean watermarked sample from token 1 onward. It may see a human-written prefix followed by model-generated text, a lightly edited generated passage embedded in a longer document, or only a late suffix that is machine-written. In those settings, early stopping is less obviously a win: the detector may spend much of its token budget before the watermark signal even begins, and whole-document validity does not automatically imply efficient localization of the suspicious span.

So I would want an evaluation where the watermarked segment starts late or occupies only part of the document. That would make it much clearer whether Anchored E-Watermarking is only a more sample-efficient detector for clean generated outputs, or whether it is also practically useful for the mixed, partially edited documents that matter most in real provenance audits.
